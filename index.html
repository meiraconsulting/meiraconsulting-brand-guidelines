01Voice

How we sound.

Calm, specific, and senior. We write like the operator in the room — not the vendor at the door.

Plain not technical

Use the precise word, then define it in passing. Jargon is a last resort, never a flex.

Embedded not detached

We write from your side of the table. “With you,” never “for you.” The plural is the point.

Accountable not advisory

We name what we will own and when it goes live. A plan is a promise, not a hand-off.

Specific not vague

Name the process, the system, the number. Specifics earn the trust that adjectives can’t.

We don’t hand you a plan. We see it through.

03Typography

Two families. Sans only.

Roboto sets the headlines; Inter carries the reading. The same two faces the product ships with — nothing else.

Aa

Roboto

Display and headings. Set at Regular 400 with tight tracking; Bold 700 for the one word that carries the accent.

04Wordmark

The mark.

The Meira wordmark with the staircase climbing out of the corner — the steps point the way up.

Primary lockup · on dark
Clear space
Minimum size Hold the logo at 96px wide or larger.

Don’t

Don’t place the white wordmark on a light background, tilt it, or add effects. Don’t recolor the staircase into two accents at once, and never detach the steps from the name.

05Marks

The staircase.

Three brackets climbing from the corner — Meira’s mark for maturity, one step at a time. Quiet punctuation, never wallpaper.

Specimen · primary mark  —  one weight, square caps, three steps. No fills, no gradients, no rotation.

Anatomy

Equal insets between steps. Square caps. Three concentric brackets — never more, never fewer.

Cluster

Two or three, varied in scale and climbing — never lined up flat. Editorial, not mechanical.

Scale

Small as an accent, medium as a pause, large as a quiet headline.

Rule

One cluster per view. Pair it with the wordmark or a headline — never with body copy. Color: a single accent, usually green or magenta.
